Frontiers of Finance

Frontiers of Finance is the official journal published by SYSU Advanced Institute of Finance. Frontiers of Finance publishes high quality original manuscripts spanning all the major fields of finance. Areas of interest include, but not limited to ITFIN, Sci-Tech Finance, financial legislation, monetary policies, financial reform. Frontiers of Finance emphasizes integrates theory of finance and practice, and focuses on empirical, applied, policy-oriented research in domestic and international financial institutions and markets. It also raises concern over reform and development of domestic and global financial market and economics by exploring practical policies, analyzing financial operation. Frontiers of Finance provides a forum for the exchange of ideas and techniques among academicians, practitioners and policymakers in the area of finance, widely transmitting comprehensive and accurate financial information and knowledge. Frontiers of Finance addresses a readership in business, academia and politicians, welcomes contributions for publication from the following categories: original comprehensive reviews of economics, case studies, preliminary findings and high-quality research paper.

Financial Innovation

Financial innovation offers a global forum for exchanging innovative research findings across all fields of financial research. Areas of interest include asset pricing, derivative pricing and hedging, high-frequency and algorithmic trading, disruptive models and more.

Journal of Management Science and Engineering

Journal of Management Science and Engineering (JMSE) is a scholarly journal that publishes scientific research on the latest improvement and practice of management science and engineering, an area emphasizing modeling, optimization, computation and data analytics in identifying and solving management problems, making business decisions, and managing risks in complex management systems. JMSE strives to stimulate the researches resulting from the collision of technological disruption, changing atlas of the world economy, and common challenges human being are facing today. It serves the entire management science and engineering community including researchers, practitioners, educators and students. We encourages the studies addressing the research questions abstracted from management and business practice, the works focusing on new management topics brought by social and technology advancement, as well as the interdisciplinary research and cross-functional issues in management and business. Both empirical and theoretical contributions are welcome.
