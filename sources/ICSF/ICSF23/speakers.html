Professor Bohui Zhang

Professor Bohui Zhang is Presidential Chair Professor of Finance and Executive Dean of School of Management and Economics at The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen. He is also the associate director of Shenzhen Institute of Data Economy, and the director of the Center for FinTech and Social Finance at Shenzhen Finance Institute. Before joining CUHK Shenzhen, he was the professor of finance at UNSW Business School, UNSW Sydney, and the associate director of the Institute of Global Finance. He studies the role of information intermediaries on capital markets, Chinese and foreign capital markets, and Fintech. His papers have been accepted for publication in the global top-tier finance, accounting, and business journals. He has also been awarded with research grants from Australian Research Council, the Centre for International Finance and Regulation, Australian School of Business, and National Natural Science Foundation of China.

Professor Zied Ftiti

Professor Zied Ftiti is full professor of Financial Economics, and Associate Dean of Research at EDC Paris Business School. After completing his master’s degree in Money, Finance, and International Economics (Option of Applied Macroeconomics) at University of Lyon 2, he has obtained his PhD in Economics in University of Lyon (GATE Laboratory, CNRS-UMR 5824) and a PhD in Business in the University of Tunis, High Institute of Management. In 2013, he obtained Qualification (“habiliation”) for Supervising Doctoral Research in management sciences, from University of Tunis, and in 2019 he obtained Qualification (“habiliation”) for supervising doctoral in Economics in University of CY Cergy Paris University. His research area includes Macroeconomics (monetary policy, capital flows, inflation, etc.), International Finance (behavioral finance, financial markets, alternative investment & markets), Financial Econometrics (time series analysis, frequency analysis and high frequency data). 

Professor Jing Wu

Professor Jing Wu is a tenured associate professor at CUHK Business School, specializing in the operations-finance interface, global supply chains, FinTech, and business intelligence. He holds a Ph.D. and MBA from the University of Chicago Booth School of Business and a bachelor's degree in Electronic Engineering from Tsinghua University. Prof. Wu has received multiple best paper awards in OM and IS fields, with his research published in renowned journals like Management Science, M&SOM, and POMS. In particular, his quantitative research findings on the supply chain impact of COVID-19 and the Trade War have been reported by over 400 media outlets in over 20 countries worldwide. He is also a Senior Editor for Production and Operations Management and serves on the Editorial Review Board for the Journal of Operations Management. Before entering academia, he worked as a quantitative strategist at Deutsche Bank New York.

Dr Ye Luo

Dr Ye Luo received his Ph.D from Massachusetts Institute of Technology at year 2015. He received his B.S. degree from Massachusetts Institute of Technology at year 2010, majored in Mathematics and Economics. Before joining FBE of HKU, he worked as assistant professor at the economics department in University of Florida. Dr. Ye Luo’s main research interests include high dimensional econometrics/statistics, machine learning and its empirical applications in economics and finance, for example, applying machine learning and A.I. in default risk prediction, algorithmic trading, dynamic demand prediction, etc. He also has interest and expertise in natural language processing.

Rachel Woolley

Rachel is a financial crime subject-matter expert, with particular expertise in AML/CTF legislation and financial crime typologies. Working for some of the world's largest financial institutions, Rachel began her career as a compliance officer, focusing on anti-money laundering and broader risk management.
